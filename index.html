Key Metrics
P/E RatioPRICE-TO-EARNINGSHow much investors pay per $1 of profit. P/E of 20 means the stock costs 20× annual earnings. Lower = cheaper, but context matters — growth stocks command higher P/Es.
—
EPS (TTM)EARNINGS PER SHARE (TRAILING 12M)Company profit divided by shares outstanding over the last 12 months. Higher EPS = more profitable per share. Negative EPS means the company is currently losing money.
—
52W High52-WEEK HIGHThe highest price the stock traded in the past year. If current price is near the high, the stock is in strong momentum. A breakout above the 52W high is often a bullish signal.
—
52W Low52-WEEK LOWThe lowest price traded in the past year. If price is near the low, the stock may be in distress or undervalued — check fundamentals to distinguish the two.
—
Mkt CapMARKET CAPITALISATIONTotal market value of all shares = share price × shares outstanding. Large-cap (>$10B) are more stable; small-cap (<$2B) are higher risk/reward.
—
BetaBETA (VOLATILITY VS MARKET)How much the stock moves relative to the market. Beta 1.5 = moves 1.5× the S&P 500. Higher beta = more volatile. Used to size positions and calculate required margin of safety.
—
FMP RatingANALYST CONSENSUS RATINGAggregated buy/hold/sell rating from Wall Street analysts via Financial Modeling Prep. Strong Buy > Buy > Hold > Sell. Consensus data — not a single analyst opinion.
—
Analyst TargetANALYST PRICE TARGETAverage 12-month price target from Wall Street analysts. Upside = how far above current price. Used in our recommendation engine alongside intrinsic value calculations.

Options Plays
📈 CALL · BullishCALL OPTIONThe right to BUY 100 shares at the strike price before expiry. Profits when the stock rises above the strike + premium paid. Max loss = premium only.
—
—
🛡 PUT · HedgePUT OPTIONThe right to SELL 100 shares at the strike price. Used to hedge downside or bet bearish. Profits when the stock falls below strike − premium paid.
—
—
⚖️ Bull SpreadBULL CALL SPREADBuy one call, sell a higher strike call. Reduces cost vs outright call. Defined max gain & max loss. Best for moderate bullish moves — not moonshots.
—
—
🌡 IV RankIMPLIED VOLATILITY RANKWhere current IV sits vs its 52-week range. 0% = lowest IV seen, 100% = highest. High IVR → sell premium (Covered Calls, CSPs). Low IVR → buy options (Long Calls, LEAPS).
